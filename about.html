Hi! I'm an upper-year student of Engineering Physics at McMaster University. I am always seeking opportunities to apply my skills and knowledge of thermal and optical product development.

Experience

During my undergrad, I've taken two industrial work terms: a 4-month summer student position at L3Harris WESCAM, and a 15-month Thermal Design internship at Christie Digital Systems.

My experience at WESCAM was formative, and where I discovered my interest in photonics technologies. In addition to the operational work of processing design changes and calibrating new laboratory equipment, I had the opportunity to perform a research project on potential applications of Adaptive Optics in the industry! This technology is incredibly cool, and I actually had the opportunity to work with it in the context of Digital Micromirror Devices at my next work placement.

The following year, I had the opportunity to join Christie Digital for a much longer period. At this job, my interests really fell into place. I got to develop a lot of technical skills I had only been introduced to before: CAD and rapid prototyping, optical and thermal instrumentation, feedback-controlled test automation using Python, and data analysis using VBA in Excel. Given that I was a student, I was given an incredible amount of freedom to work on projects that interested me, and choose the skills I wanted to develop. After a few months in this job, I was regularly applying advanced mathematical and physical concepts to my work every day.
