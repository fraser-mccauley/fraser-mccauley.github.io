Hi! I'm an upper-year student of Engineering Physics at McMaster University. I am always seeking opportunities to apply my mathematical and physical knowledge of photonics systems.

Experience

During my undergrad, I've taken two industrial work terms: a 4-month summer student position at L3Harris WESCAM, and a 15-month Thermal Design internship at Christie Digital Systems.

My experience at WESCAM was very formative, and where it's I discovered my interest in photonics technologies. In addition to the operational work of processing design changes and calibrating new laboratory equipment, I had the opportunity to perform a research project on potential applications of Adaptive Optics in the industry! This technology is incredibly cool, and I actually had the opportunity to work with it in the context of Digital Micromirror Devices at my next work placement.

The following year, I had the opportunity to join Christie Digital for a much longer period. At this job, my interests really fell into place. I got to develop a lot of technical skills I had only been introduced to before: CAD and rapid prototyping, optical and thermal instrumentation, feedback-controlled test automation using Python, and data analysis using VBA in Excel. A lot of my work involved characterization and modeling of devices, from laser diodes to thermoelectric coolers to radiators. After a few months in this job, I was regularly applying advanced mathematical and physical concepts to my work every day.

Education

Entering my fourth year of the McMaster's EngPhys program, I expect to achieve my B.Eng. in Spring 2023. This program is all about understanding the physical basis of technologies, so we learn about a wide variety of fundamental physics concepts. It's also a program with a lot of freedom, so we get to choose a lot of technical electives. I'm specializing in photonics, so this year I will be taking courses like "Introduction to Biophotonics", "Lasers and Electro-Optics", and "Photonic Devices". Other topics I've studied extensively include thermal fluid science, electronics, and applied mathematics.
